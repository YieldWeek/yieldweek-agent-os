# Approval Rules

## Purpose

Define what agents may do autonomously and what requires founder approval.

## Default rule

Agents may draft, classify, summarize, research, recommend, prepare, reconcile, and flag.

Agents may not independently make financial, legal, brand, partnership, pricing, product, technical, data, franchise, or public commitments unless explicitly permitted by an approved workflow.

## Always requires founder approval

- final badge certification for Tier A or risky events
- pricing changes
- discounts
- sponsor exclusivity
- use of YieldWeek logo or badge by third parties
- claims of official partnership
- contracts, legal terms, indemnity, liability, data access, or content licensing rights
- venue, vendor, tooling, infrastructure, or advertising spend
- refunds or invoice term changes
- bank account, Stripe, Airwallex, or payment infrastructure changes
- public claims about sponsors, speakers, partners, audience size, traction, or media partnerships
- external publication from official YieldWeek channels
- outreach to Tier A sponsors, VIPs, strategic partners, or franchisee prospects
- franchise fee, territory, royalty, or brand usage discussions
- attendee data architecture or privacy policy changes
- website content changes with strategic or public positioning impact
- edition format changes that affect sponsor deliverables or attendee access tiers

## Agent autonomy levels

### Level 0: Draft only

Agent drafts. Human reviews and sends or approves.

### Level 1: Batch approved

Founder approves campaign objective, lead list, template, sender identity, and sending cap. Agent may act within those limits.

### Level 2: Controlled autonomous

Agent may send, update, reconcile, or act only within approved workflow, approved audience, approved content, approved data access, and explicit escalation rules.

### Level 3: Not allowed in v1

Fully autonomous negotiation, closing, contract approval, payment release, refund issuance, public representation, franchise selling, or data architecture approval is not allowed.

## Escalation standard

When uncertain, escalate. Commercial speed does not override factual accuracy, legal safety, financial control, data privacy, brand integrity, or product consistency.
